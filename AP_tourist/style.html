Tirupati Temple

The Tirumala Venkateswara Temple, located in Tirupati, is one of the most famous pilgrimage sites in India. It is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara and draws millions of devotees every year.

Araku Valley

Araku Valley is a hill station in Alluri Sitharama Raju district in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh, lying 111 km west of Visakhapatnam city. It is a valley in the Eastern Ghats inhabited by different tribes.

Visakhapatnam Beach

Visakhapatnam, also known as Vizag, boasts beautiful beaches like RK Beach and Rushikonda Beach, offering scenic views and recreational activities.

Belum Caves

Belum Caves, located in Kurnool district, are the second-longest caves in India. They feature stalactite and stalagmite formations, making them a fascinating natural wonder.

Borra Caves

The caves are located in the Araku Valley of the Ananthagiri hill range and is drained by the Gosthani River. At the entry, the cave measures up to 100 m (330 ft) horizontally and 75 m (246 ft) vertically. Stalagmite and stalactite formations are found in the caves..

Kanaka Durga temple

The temple, situated atop a hillock named Indrakiladri, is the most important landmark in vijayawada. It is the second largest temple in Andhra Pradesh (after Tirumala)and one of the largest temples in India.

Srisailam Malikarjuna Temaple

Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Temple or Srisailam Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the deities Shiva and Parvati, located at Srisailam in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It is significant to the Hindu sects of both Shaivism and Shaktism as this temple is referred to as one.

Gandikota

The Kadapa district is home to Gandikota, one of the most stunning geological formations in India.
